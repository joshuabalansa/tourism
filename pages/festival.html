Festivals and Events

Masskara Festival

The Masskara Festival is a vibrant and lively celebration held annually in Bacolod City, Philippines. Known for its colorful masks, street dancing, and exuberant atmosphere, the festival symbolizes the resilience and optimism of the local community in the face of challenges, making it a captivating and joyful event that draws visitors from around the world.
When: A month-long celebration annually every October with highlights held every 4th sunday.
Where: Masskara Festival is celebrated in many places of Bacolod City such as Bacolod City Government Center (BCGC), Upper East Megaworld, Bacolod Public Plaza, Old Airport, and Lacson Streets.

Panaad sa Negros

Panaad sa Negros Festival is a cultural extravaganza in Negros Occidental, Philippines, held showcasing the rich heritage and diverse traditions of the province. It is a spirited occasion that fosters unity of pride and among the people of Negros Occidental.

Where: Panaad Park and Stadium, Bacolod City

Bacolaodiat Festival (Bacolod Chinese New Year)

Bacolaodiat Festival in Bacolod City is a Chinese New Year celebration that merges traditional Chinese customs with the local culture of Negros Occidental. It came from the words Bacolod and Lao Diat, which means celebration. The festival is marked by lively street parades featuring dragon and lion dances, vibrant lantern displays, cultural performances, and a fusion of Chinese and Filipino culinary delights. It serves as a joyous and inclusive event, bringing together the community to welcome the Lunar New Year with a colorful blend of festivities and cultural exchange.
When: Held annually on January 20th
Where: San Sebastian Cathedral, Bacolod City

Feast of Saint Sebastian

The Feast of Saint Sebastian in Bacolod City is a religious celebration honoring the patron saint of the city. Devotees participate in solemn processions, church activities, and prayers, creating a spiritual atmosphere that reflects the deep religious and cultural significance of this event in Bacolod.
When: Typically celebrated during Chinese New Year, which falls between late January and mid-February.
Where: The festival takes place in various locations across Bacolod City, mostly in Lacson Streets.
Fb page/website: Bacolaodiat Festival

Inasal Festival

The renowned chicken dish of this city was featured on a 320-meter-long grilling setup along the main road of a commercial district. Around 70 vendors participated in cooking the dish to mark the conclusion of the three-day Bacolod Chicken Inasal Festival on Sunday evening. The grilling spectacle, highlighting 3,000 skewers of different chicken cuts, is deemed as the longest of its kind in the Philippines, as stated by Councilor Celia Flor, who also serves as the festival co-chairperson.
